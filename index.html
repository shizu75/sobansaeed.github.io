About Me

Engineer driven by research, innovation, and real-world impact

I am a graduate Electrical Engineer (GPA: 3.83/4.00) with research experience spanning embedded systems, signal processing, and machine-learning-based analysis of real-world data. My work emphasizes system-level design, where sensing hardware, embedded computation, and data-driven models are developed and evaluated under practical constraints such as noise, latency, and limited resources.

Through academic projects and an international research internship, I have gained experience in EEG-based brain–computer interfaces, biomedical instrumentation and circuit design, signal acquisition and preprocessing pipelines, FPGA-based control systems, and AI-assisted inference models.
